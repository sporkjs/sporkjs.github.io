Hinton was the first to coin the word "tesseract" as a name for a cube-like object he used to describe four-dimensional theories. It can be described as a cube contained within a larger cube. Mathematicians may refer to this as a 4-cube, 8-cell, hypercube, or regular octachoron.

Below left: a static tesseract. Below right: a tesseract in motion.

Hinton speculated that the Fourth Dimension was that of time and space: an unseeable, yet ever present substance. The movement of the tesseract represents both motion over time and a change in space, or volume.
