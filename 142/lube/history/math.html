Germain is most famous for her work with Number Theory.

She challenged Fermat's last theorem, which states that there is no solution to x^n + y^n = z^n if n is an integer greater than 2, and x, y, and z are nonzero integers.

Germain asserted that when x, y, z and n are prime, and n is smaller than 100, there is a solution.

This work was not published at first, and kept within Germain's personal discoveries. But after consistent contact with Legendre, her work was first seen in a supplement to his Theories des nombres.
